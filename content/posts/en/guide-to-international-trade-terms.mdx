## Introduction

When engaging in international trade, understanding Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) is crucial for smooth transactions. These standardized trade terms, published by the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC), define the responsibilities of buyers and sellers in international transactions.

This guide covers the most commonly used Incoterms 2020 and helps you choose the right terms for your business.

## Why Incoterms Matter

Incoterms clearly define:

- **Who arranges and pays for shipping**
- **Who bears the risk during transit**
- **Who handles customs clearance**
- **When ownership transfers from seller to buyer**

Using the correct Incoterms prevents disputes and ensures both parties understand their obligations.

## The 11 Incoterms 2020

### Group E - Departure

#### EXW (Ex Works)

**Seller's Responsibility:** Minimal - goods available at seller's premises

**Buyer's Responsibility:** All costs from seller's door to final destination

**Best For:** Experienced buyers with strong logistics capabilities

**Risk Transfer:** At seller's premises

> **Practical Tip:** EXW places maximum responsibility on the buyer. Ensure you have reliable freight forwarders before choosing this term.

### Group F - Main Carriage Unpaid

#### FCA (Free Carrier)

**Seller's Responsibility:** Delivers goods to carrier at named place

**Buyer's Responsibility:** Main carriage and insurance

**Best For:** Container shipments, multimodal transport

**Risk Transfer:** When goods are handed to carrier

#### FOB (Free On Board)

**Seller's Responsibility:** Loads goods onto vessel at port of shipment

**Buyer's Responsibility:** Freight, insurance, and destination costs

**Best For:** Bulk cargo, sea freight

**Risk Transfer:** Once goods pass ship's rail

> **Important:** FOB should only be used for sea and inland waterway transport.

### Group C - Main Carriage Paid

#### CFR (Cost and Freight)

**Seller's Responsibility:** Pays freight to destination port

**Buyer's Responsibility:** Insurance, destination charges, customs

**Best For:** Sea shipments when buyer wants to handle insurance

**Risk Transfer:** At port of shipment (despite seller paying freight)

#### CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight)

**Seller's Responsibility:** Freight and insurance to destination port

**Buyer's Responsibility:** Destination charges and customs

**Best For:** First-time importers, those wanting seller to arrange shipping

**Risk Transfer:** At port of shipment

> **Note:** CIF only requires minimum insurance coverage (110% of contract value). Consider additional coverage for high-value goods.

#### CPT (Carriage Paid To)

**Seller's Responsibility:** Carriage to named destination

**Buyer's Responsibility:** Insurance, destination handling

**Best For:** Air freight, multimodal transport

#### CIP (Carriage and Insurance Paid To)

**Seller's Responsibility:** Carriage and insurance to destination

**Buyer's Responsibility:** Destination handling

**Best For:** Air freight when seller arranges insurance

### Group D - Arrival

#### DAP (Delivered at Place)

**Seller's Responsibility:** Delivery to named place, excluding unloading

**Buyer's Responsibility:** Unloading, import customs, duties

**Best For:** Door-to-door delivery when buyer handles import formalities

#### DPU (Delivered at Place Unloaded)

**Seller's Responsibility:** Delivery and unloading at destination

**Buyer's Responsibility:** Import customs and duties

**Best For:** When unloading facilities are limited at destination

#### DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)

**Seller's Responsibility:** Maximum - all costs to buyer's door including duties

**Buyer's Responsibility:** Minimal - receiving goods

**Best For:** Buyers wanting turnkey delivery, e-commerce

> **Caution:** DDP requires the seller to handle import customs. Ensure your supplier is registered for import in your country or uses a capable agent.

## Choosing the Right Incoterm

### For New Importers

**Recommended:** CIF or DDP

- Seller handles most logistics
- Predictable total costs
- Reduced complexity

### For Experienced Traders

**Recommended:** FOB or FCA

- Better control over shipping
- Potential cost savings
- Flexibility in carrier selection

### For High-Value Goods

**Recommended:** CIP or DAP with additional insurance

- Comprehensive coverage
- Clear liability boundaries
- Reduced risk exposure

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using FOB for container cargo** - FCA is more appropriate
2. **Assuming CIF includes adequate insurance** - Verify coverage limits
3. **Neglecting to specify the named place** - Be precise (e.g., "FOB Shanghai Port" not just "FOB China")
4. **Mixing Incoterms with payment terms** - They are separate agreements

## Conclusion

Selecting the right Incoterm depends on your experience, logistics capabilities, and risk tolerance. When in doubt, discuss with your supplier and freight forwarder to find the optimal solution for your specific situation.

For first-time buyers, we recommend starting with CIF terms and gradually transitioning to FOB as you build relationships with reliable freight partners.
